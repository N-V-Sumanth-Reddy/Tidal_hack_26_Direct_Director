## Ad Concept: **“Build Time”**  
**Theme:** A TAMU Tidal Hackathon attendee daydreams into a time-travel “machine” and lands at the Taj Mahal during its construction.

### The Big Idea  
Turn “time travel” into a metaphor for what hacking really does: **compress centuries of progress into a weekend**. The attendee doesn’t just visit history—they *collaborate with it*, realizing that the Taj Mahal wasn’t magic; it was an audacious build powered by people, planning, iteration, and obsession with craft—exactly what a hackathon is.

---

## Story / Film (60–90s Hero Spot)
**Open (Present Day, TAMU Tidal Hackathon):**  
A sleep-deprived attendee stares at their laptop: CAD mockups, code, a failing demo. The stage lights blur. The emcee says, “You’ve got 24 hours to build something impossible.”

**The “Machine”:**  
Their **conference badge lanyard** swings like a pendulum. The badge QR code glitches and becomes a swirling blueprint animation. The laptop fan whirs into a low “portal” hum. The screen fills with white marble textures and gridlines—like a 3D model rendering that becomes real.

**Cut to (Agra, 1630s—Taj under construction):**  
They stumble onto a bustling construction site: scaffolding, brick cores, marble inlay work, calligraphy panels being positioned. A master builder hands them a rolled plan—visual language parallels a hackathon spec doc.

**The Twist (Collaboration Across Time):**  
They start “debugging” the build: pointing out alignment using their phone’s level, sketching a workflow, helping solve a logistics bottleneck. In return, the artisans show them craftsmanship principles: precision, patience, iteration, systems thinking.

**Climax:**  
A half-finished dome silhouette matches the hackathon attendee’s unfinished prototype back at TAMU. The builder says (subtitled):  
“Great works aren’t finished in a day. They’re finished by teams who refuse to stop.”

**Return (Present Day):**  
They snap awake. Their prototype is still broken—until they reframe it with what they learned: simplify, modularize, iterate. The demo finally runs.

**End Frame / Tagline:**  
**“Hack Time. Build the Impossible.”**  
*TAMU Tidal Hackathon — Where centuries of ambition fit in a weekend.*

## Fresh Visual Hook (Key Novel Element)
### **“Blueprint-Portal UX”**
The time machine isn’t a DeLorean or sci‑fi device. It’s a **hackathon interface**:  
- QR badge → spinning parametric blueprint  
- Laptop fan + fluorescent lights → “portal” sound design  
- UI gridlines overlay onto real-world scaffolding  
This makes it feel native to the hackathon world and avoids cliché time-travel tropes.

---

## Why This Works (Justification)
1. **Emotionally sticky contrast:** fluorescent hackathon chaos vs. monumental historical build creates instant awe.  
2. **Reframes history as engineering:** Taj Mahal construction becomes a parallel to shipping a prototype—planning, material constraints, iteration, collaboration.  
3. **Aspirational identity message:** attendees aren’t just students; they’re part of a lineage of builders.  
4. **Ownable metaphor:** “time travel” = “innovation acceleration,” a clean takeaway for an event brand.
